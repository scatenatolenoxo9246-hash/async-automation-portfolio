# Lead Sourcing Playbook

Goal: find public, relevant, contactable leads without fake identities or spam behavior.

## Lead pools

### Pool A: Shopify small agencies

Best fit:

- Shopify partners
- Ecommerce migration agencies
- Product catalog cleanup services
- Dropshipping or DTC operations consultants

Public signals to look for:

- "Shopify migration"
- "product setup"
- "catalog management"
- "bulk import"
- "store maintenance"
- "ecommerce operations"

Potential pain:

- Client product CSVs are messy.
- Supplier catalogs need cleanup.
- Product descriptions, tags, variants, and SEO fields need bulk editing.
- Agency has more implementation work than its small team can handle.

Offer angle:

> I can help with async product data cleanup and import-ready Shopify CSV preparation.

### Pool B: Zapier/Make/Airtable automation consultants

Best fit:

- Zapier experts
- Make.com partners
- Airtable consultants
- Operations automation consultants
- No-code agencies

Public signals to look for:

- "Zapier workflows"
- "Make.com scenarios"
- "Airtable systems"
- "API integration"
- "webhooks"
- "operations automation"

Potential pain:

- Client workflows require custom scripts.
- Data needs to be cleaned before automation works.
- Agencies need reliable implementation capacity.
- Client handoff docs take time.

Offer angle:

> I can help with async implementation support for API scripts, webhook helpers, CSV cleanup, and handoff documentation.

### Pool C: small marketing or website agencies

Best fit:

- Webflow/WordPress agencies
- Local SEO agencies
- Small B2B website studios
- Growth and performance marketing agencies

Public signals to look for:

- "lead generation"
- "landing pages"
- "CRM"
- "forms"
- "reporting"
- "client dashboards"

Potential pain:

- Leads from forms are not routed cleanly.
- Reports are manual.
- CRM data is messy.
- Clients ask for small automations outside the agency's main scope.

Offer angle:

> I can help with small backend scripts, form-to-CRM cleanup, reporting automation, and support FAQ prototypes.

## Where to find leads

Use official/public sources first:

- Shopify partner directory: `https://www.shopify.com/partners/directory`
- Zapier experts directory: `https://zapier.com/experts`
- Make partner directory: `https://www.make.com/en/partners-directory`
- Airtable partners: `https://www.airtable.com/partners`
- Webflow experts: `https://webflow.com/experts`
- Clutch agency listings: `https://clutch.co`
- LinkedIn company search
- Agency portfolio pages
- GitHub profiles for small tool builders
- Indie Hackers or SaaS communities where contact info is public


## Qualification checklist

Add a lead only if at least 4 of these are true:

- It is a small team or founder-led agency.
- It sells services to clients.
- It has a clear contact route.
- Its public website mentions data, migration, automation, operations, CRM, or ecommerce implementation.
- It appears active in the last 12 months.
- You can write one specific opening line based on public information.

## Reject immediately

- Huge enterprise consultancies.
- No contact route.
- Job-only hiring page with no partner/vendor route.
- Crypto, adult, gambling, or high-risk regulated work unless you understand compliance.
- Any lead that requires fake identity, fake location, or fake credentials.

## Daily sourcing target

Start small:

- Find 15 raw leads.
- Score them.
- Keep the best 8-10.
- Send 5-10 personalized messages.
- Log every touch in `lead_tracker.csv`.

Quality target:

- Every sent message should include one public signal specific to that lead.
